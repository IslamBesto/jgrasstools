Subdivides the sites of a basin in 11 topographic classes, nine of which are deﬁned according to [1].

Such classes are the nine classes based (also in [2]) obtained with TC ; the points belonging to the channel networks constitute a tenth class (derived from the use of ExtractNetwork), the points with high slope (higher than a critical angle) the eleventh class.

References:

[1] Suzuki R. Slope proﬁles and classiﬁcation of slope types. Surveying no. 7, 43-50, 1977. [2] Parsons A. Hillslope form. Routledge, First edition, 1988.
